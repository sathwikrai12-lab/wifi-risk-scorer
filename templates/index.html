🛡️ How to protect yourself on public Wi-Fi
Follow these steps every time you connect to a public network
1
Always use a VPN
A VPN encrypts all your traffic and hides your real IP address. Even on a dangerous open network, a VPN makes your data unreadable to attackers.
Recommended: Mullvad, ProtonVPN, or Windscribe (free)
2
Only visit HTTPS websites
Check for the padlock icon in your browser. HTTPS means the website encrypts data between you and the server. Never enter passwords on HTTP sites.
Install HTTPS Everywhere browser extension for automatic upgrades
3
Turn off auto-connect
Disable your device's auto-connect feature. Hackers set up fake hotspots with common names like "Free Airport WiFi" to trick your device into connecting automatically.
Settings → Wi-Fi → disable "Auto-join" on unknown networks
4
Enable your firewall
Your device's firewall blocks unauthorized incoming connections. Keep it enabled especially on public networks where other users could probe your device.
Windows: Control Panel → Firewall | Mac: System Settings → Firewall
5
Avoid sensitive tasks
Never do online banking, enter credit card details, or log into important accounts on public Wi-Fi. Wait until you're on a trusted network or use mobile data instead.
Use mobile hotspot for anything sensitive — it's much safer
6
Use two-factor authentication
Enable 2FA on all your accounts. Even if someone steals your password on a public network, they cannot log in without your second factor (phone or authenticator app).
Use Google Authenticator or Authy — avoid SMS 2FA when possible
7
Forget the network after use
After using a public network, tell your device to forget it. This prevents your device from automatically reconnecting to that network (or a fake copy of it) in the future.
Wi-Fi settings → tap network name → Forget / Remove
